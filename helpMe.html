This model has 2 parts - the ANSWER model (which calculates water use or yield as a function of soil, crop, climate, irrigation water salinity and irrigation water amount) and the economic model (which calculates farm profits based on the results of ANSWER). In order to receive output from the model you must do the following:

Part 1:

(1) Choose a crop type:

     Each crop has 6 parameters that can be edited manually. Tp (potential transpiration) is average daily crop transpiration (mm). EC50 and p characterize the sigmoidal crop response curve to salinity where EC50 is the soil saturated paste solution electrical conductivity causing 50% yield decrease and p is an exponent term often assumed to = 3 for agricultural crops. Psi root (Ψroot) is a measure of water head at the root soil interface; Ψroot = (-) 6000 mm is likely appropriate for most crops. b is the effective distance between roots and soil and will often be assumed to be 10 mm. Yr0 is a fitting term to consider yield expected with zero irrigation.

(2) Choose a soil type:

     Each soil type has 6 parameters that can be edited manually. Each soil type has 6 parameters that can be edited manually. Ks is saturated hydraulic conductivity, theta_s (θs) is saturated soil water content and theta_r (θr) is residual soil water content). beta (β), eta (η) and delta (δ) are empirical parameters of the Brooks-Corey soil hydraulic model where δ=ηβ-1. Psi water (Ψwater) is soil air entry head.

(3) Fix parameters to suit your inquiry:

     (a) Water salinity - the range (minimum to maximum) and step size (increments in the range), can be set manually.

     (b) Irrigation - the range (minimum to maximum) and step size (increments in the range), can be set manually. Maximum irrigation is set initially to be 2 times Tp.

(4) Calculate relative yield:

     Press the 'Calculate yield' button to plot the ANSWER model relative yield, as a function of both water salinity and irrigation. You may export the plotted data to an EXCEL file using the 'Export to Excel' button.

Part 2:

(5) Water pricing:

     You may choose either 'Fresh water' or 'Brackish water'.

     Choosing fresh water exhibits a uniform price for all salinity values; the price can be edited manually. Brackish water have different values for different salinities, which may also be edited.

(6) Quota deviation:

     In order to take into account quota deviation you must check the 'include quota deviation' box.

     (a) Fresh water - fixed price is set for up to 30% deviation, and for above 30% deviation (can be edited).

     (b) Brackish water - precentage of price increase for up to 8% deviation, and for above 8% deviation (can be edited).

(7) Economic parameters:

     (a) Dunam/Hectare amount - set the size of the farm/field in appropriate units.

     (b) Revenue (per ton) - the seasonal revenue of the given crop per ton.

     (c) Return (per dunam/hectare) - yearly return on capital investment, with 7% interest.

     (d) Yield dependent costs (per ton) - set the yield dependent costs.

     (e) Fixed costs (per dunam/hectare) - fixed costs, such as - labor, energy, pesticides, etc.

     (f) Season length - the length of the crop's season, in months.

(8) Calculate the profit:

     Press the 'Calculate profit' button to plot the estimated profit, as a function of both water salinity and irrigation. You may export the plotted data to an EXCEL file using the 'Export to Excel' button.

(9) Reset:

     In order to reset to default values, reload the page in the browser (refresh button).

(10) NIS/USD:

     Changing the currency from NIS to USD can be done upon entry page of the application. The value of the currency rate can be set manually (you may use this feature to mimic other currencies).

(11) Dunam/Hectare:

     Use the option to change from dunam to hectare (and vice versa), at the entry page of the application.

The Analytical Salt-Water (ANSWER) model (Shani et al., 2007, 2009) is a one-dimensional mechanistic-based tool, governed by physical and biological soil-plant-atmosphere processes, that considers multiple environmental variables and stressors and their combined effect on plant response, expressed as water use or biomass production. The ANSWER model captures essential factors of the soil-plant-atmospheric system as a closed form analytical solution. The analytical approach is based on four assumptions: environmental conditions in the root zone affecting plant growth and uptake can be represented by effective values of relevant parameters including water content and soil salinity (expressed as EC), conditions are steady state with respect to water and salt status (thus there are no time dimensions), a proportional relationship exists between the ratio of yield to potential yield which relates to biomass production, and finally that the climate conditions are static. The ANSWER model consists of a water balance, a salt balance, a hydraulic water content and water movement model (Brooks and Corey 1964, 1966), a root water uptake response model (Nimah and Hanks 1973), and the van Genuchten and Gupta (1993) uptake reduction due to salinity function. These allow the consideration of management (irrigation water quantity and quality), biological (plant response), and physical (soil, water and weather) variables in a closed form equation. The ANSWER model is presented, explained and applied in Shani et al (2007, 2009) and has been further applied in a number of published agronomic, horticultural, environmental and economic studies (Ben-Gal 2007; Ben-Gal et al., 2008; Ben-Gal et al., 2009; Ben-Gal et al., 2013; Ben-Gal et al., 2011; Hadas et al., 2016).
